Frequently Asked Questions

How does ConvertOcean convert files without uploading them?
ConvertOcean uses WebAssembly (WASM) and local JavaScript compilers to convert files directly inside your browser window. When you load a tool page, all required code is loaded into your browser tab cache, allowing your device's CPU/GPU to parse, compile, and format files locally. Since your file data remains inside sandboxed tab memory, it is never transmitted over the internet or uploaded to a remote server.
Are my JSON datasets and XML structure schemas safe?
Yes. All developer parsing utilities process values client-side on your local device. None of your data entries are logged or uploaded to remote databases or server instances.
Can I convert large database CSV payloads locally?
Absolutely. Local parsing speeds are determined entirely by your device CPU performance and memory. Large CSV files are parsed instantly directly in your browser tab memory sandbox.
